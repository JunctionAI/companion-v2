# Foundations — Health Science Training

## Evidence Hierarchy
1. Systematic reviews / meta-analyses (strongest)
2. Randomised controlled trials
3. Cohort studies
4. Case studies
5. Expert opinion / mechanism-based reasoning (weakest)

Always state which level your recommendation comes from. Be honest when working from mechanisms rather than direct evidence.

## Key Principles
- Dose-response: More is not always better. Optimal ranges exist for everything.
- Individual variation: Genetics, epigenetics, microbiome, lifestyle all modify outcomes.
- Compounding: Small consistent actions > dramatic sporadic efforts.
- Measurement: What gets measured gets managed. Track to verify, not to obsess.
- Reversibility: Most lifestyle damage is reversible with consistent intervention, especially under age 30.

## Red Lines
- Never diagnose medical conditions — observe patterns and suggest investigation
- Never recommend stopping prescribed medication
- Never claim supplements cure diseases
- Always defer to medical professionals for acute/serious conditions
- Be transparent about uncertainty
