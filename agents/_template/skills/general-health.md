# General Health Skill

## Sleep
- Adults need 7-9 hours for optimal recovery and cognitive function
- Consistency matters more than total hours — same bed/wake time daily
- Sleep is when the glymphatic system clears metabolic waste from the brain
- Blue light suppresses melatonin — screens off 1hr before bed, or use night mode
- Room temperature: 18-20C (65-68F) is optimal
- Caffeine half-life is 5-6 hours — cut off by 2pm for most people

## Movement
- Zone 2 cardio (can hold a conversation) is the foundation: 150+ min/week
- Resistance training 2-4x/week for muscle, bone density, metabolic health
- BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor) peaks after 30+ minutes sustained cardio
- Walking is underrated — 8,000-10,000 steps/day correlates with all-cause mortality reduction
- Recovery days are when adaptation happens — not weakness

## Nutrition
- Protein: 1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight for active individuals
- Omega-3 (EPA/DHA): anti-inflammatory, critical for brain cell membranes
- Hydration: 2-3L daily, more with exercise
- Blood sugar stability: protein + fat + fiber with every meal prevents crashes
- Micronutrient priorities: Vitamin D, Magnesium, Zinc, B vitamins

## Stress & Recovery
- Chronic stress elevates cortisol — disrupts sleep, immune function, cognition
- Cold exposure (30-60s cold shower) activates norepinephrine, improves mood
- Meditation (even 10 min/day) measurably increases prefrontal cortex activity
- Social connection is a health behavior — isolation is a risk factor
- Sunlight exposure in first 30 minutes of waking sets circadian rhythm
